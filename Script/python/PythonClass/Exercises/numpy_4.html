(a) Conditional Evaluation

Start by creating an array representing a range of values from -5 to 5.

>>> a = numpy.random.uniform(-1,1,10)
>>> a
array([ 0.37933235, -0.12286626, -0.24445765,  0.85411263,  0.66843112,
       -0.14355917, -0.57310431,  0.65532876,  0.39393187, -0.37267529])
>>>

Perform a calculation: 

>>> numpy.sqrt(a)
array([ 0.61589963,         NaN,         NaN,  0.92418214,  0.81757637,
               NaN,         NaN,  0.80952378,  0.62763992,         NaN])
>>>

Instead of NaN, suppose you wanted to have all of the negative values evaluate to 0. Try this:

>>> a < 0
array([False,  True,  True, False, False,  True,  True, False, False,  True], dtype=bool)
>>> numpy.where(a<0, 0, numpy.sqrt(a))
array([ 0.61589963,  0.        ,  0.        ,  0.92418214,  0.81757637,
        0.        ,  0.        ,  0.80952378,  0.62763992,  0.        ])
>>>

(b) Operations on neighboring points

In the previous exercise, you computed following: 

>>> xpts = numpy.arange(-2,2,0.25)
>>> xpts
array([-2.  , -1.75, -1.5 , -1.25, -1.  , -0.75, -0.5 , -0.25,  0.  ,
        0.25,  0.5 ,  0.75,  1.  ,  1.25,  1.5 ,  1.75])
>>> ypts = 2*numpy.cos(2*xpts)-3.5*numpy.sin(3*xpts)
>>> ypts
array([-2.28524149, -4.8791841 , -5.40134041, -3.60275185, -0.33837364,
        2.86473059,  4.57183706,  4.14090078,  2.        , -0.63057054,
       -2.41062784, -2.58178179, -1.3262137 ,  0.39817738,  1.44137042,
        1.13335735])
>>>

Approximate the derivative of the function values in ypts doing this calculation (equivalent to (f(x+dx)-f(x))/dx):

>>> dypts = (ypts[1:] - ypts[:-1])/(xpts[1:] - xpts[:-1])
>>> dypts
array([-10.37577046,  -2.08862521,   7.19435423,  13.05751281,
        12.81241695,   6.82842589,  -1.72374512,  -8.56360314,
       -10.52228215,  -7.12022922,  -0.68461578,   5.02227234,
         6.89756434,   4.17277214,  -1.23205226])
>>>
